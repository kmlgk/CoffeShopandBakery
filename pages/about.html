How It Started

A Roaster, an Oven,
and a Lot of Heart

Brew & Crumb began in 2011 when Nora, a barista with a battered espresso machine, and Sam, a baker who couldn't sit still, took over a tiny shopfront on Kettle Street. The plan was simple: serve the kind of coffee and bread they'd want themselves, to the people next door.

Fourteen years later, not much has changed. We still roast our own beans in small batches, still mix the sourdough by hand each night, and still know most of our regulars by name — and by their usual order.

We're proud to be a genuine "third place": not home, not work, just a warm room where the neighbourhood slows down for a few minutes and feels a little more human.

What We Stand For

Our House Values

Four things we won't compromise on, no matter how busy the morning rush gets.

01

Source Kindly

Direct trade, fair prices, and long relationships with the farms behind our beans.

02

Make by Hand

Roasted, mixed, shaped, and pulled by people who care — never on autopilot.

03

Waste Less

Compostable cups, day-old bakes to the shelter, and spent grounds for the community garden.

04

Welcome All

A seat for everyone, a fair wage for our team, and a warm hello every single time.

Milestones

The Journey So Far

2011

The Doors Open

A one-machine espresso bar and a home oven. Twelve seats and a lot of optimism.

2015

We Start Roasting

Our vintage drum roaster arrives. We begin sourcing green coffee direct from farms.

2019

The Bakery Grows Up

A dedicated bake kitchen opens next door, doubling our sourdough and pastry output.

2023

Bean Subscriptions Launch

Fresh-roasted beans now ship to homes across the country, roasted the day they leave us.

2025

Still on the Corner

Same street, same values — now with a warm room for reservations and community events.
